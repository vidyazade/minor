Bacterial blight is a disease caused by bacteria that can affect many plants, including barley, wheat, oats, field peas, soybeans, and various fruit trees:

Symtoms

Bacterial blight can cause dark brown spots with yellow halos on leaves, leaf curling, and black streaks on twigs. In severe cases, it can kill leaves, blossoms, and branch tips.
Causes
The bacteria that causes bacterial blight is common in soil and water, and is spread by wind-driven rain. It can also survive on crop residue, seed, and perennial grasses.
Control
Here are some ways to control bacterial blight:
  • Crop rotation: Can help prevent inoculum from spreading from infected crops.
  • Sowing disease-free seed: Can help prevent the spread of the.
  • Using resistant varieties: Can help prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Avoiding pruning in winter: Can help prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Using copper bactericides: Can help prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Limiting cultivation to dry foliage: Can help prevent the spread of the disease.
